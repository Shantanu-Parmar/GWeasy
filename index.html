Project Overview

GWeasy is a multi-platform GUI software that simplifies gravitational wave (GW) data analysis. It integrates LIGO data fetching, OMICRON pipeline execution, and vibrant visualization, making GW research accessible to scientists and enthusiasts alike.

Developed by Shantanusinh Parmar, GWeasy eliminates the complexity of setting up GW analysis environments and softwares. Whether you’re fetching data from LIGO, running OMICRON pipelines, or visualizing spacetime ripples, GWeasy streamlines the process with an intuitive interface.

Explore our Usage page or watch our YouTube Walkthrough to dive into Gravitational analysis with GWeasy. [NO PROGRAMING SKILLS REQUIRED]

Why GWeasy?

Gravitational wave research has been transformative, but the lack of intuitive visual tools and user-friendly interfaces often poses a barrier for early-career researchers and newcomers to the field. Complex command-line setups and steep learning curves can discourage exploration of LIGO data and analysis pipelines like OMICRON.

GWeasy aims to revolutionize this landscape by offering a no-code solution. With its vibrant GUI, GWeasy empowers users to fetch, analyze, and visualize gravitational wave data without needing extensive programming expertise. By democratizing access to GW research, GWeasy fosters inclusivity and accelerates scientific discovery for students, educators, and researchers new to the field.
